Pratapgad Fort

"A legendary fort where history and bravery converged in the heart of Maharashtra."

Pratapgad Fort is a historic hill fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. Built in 1656 by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, it is one of the most significant forts of the Maratha Empire. Famous for its role in the Battle of Pratapgad in 1659, the fort remains a monument of military history and Maratha pride.

The fort's strategic location offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. It played a pivotal role in the victory over Afzal Khan, a commander of the Bijapur Sultanate, and is known for the historic duel between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan.
