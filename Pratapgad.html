Pratap Gad

Pratapgad Fort is a historic hill fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India.

Situated around 24 kilometers from the hill station of Mahabaleshwar, the fort stands at an elevation of approximately 1,080 meters (3,540 feet) above sea level and is one of the most significant forts in the Maratha Empire. It is especially famous for its connection to the Maratha ruler Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and the legendary Battle of Pratapgad that took place in 1659.

The fort was built in 1656 by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj to consolidate his control over the surrounding areas and to fortify his empire’s defenses. It was constructed by the Maratha engineer Moropant Trimbak Pingale under Shivaji Maharaj's supervision.

Pratapgad Fort is known for its strategic location, as it provides a commanding view of the surrounding valleys and mountains, including the famous Konkan coast. Its position made it an ideal location for monitoring enemy movements, which added to its military importance.

Pratapgad Fort played a pivotal role in the Battle of Pratapgad, a decisive conflict between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan, a commander of the Bijapur Sultanate. In 1659, Afzal Khan, who had been sent by the Bijapur Sultanate to defeat Shivaji Maharaj, marched toward the fort with a large army. However, Shivaji Maharaj, with his military acumen, lured Afzal Khan into a trap.
The battle was primarily fought near the fort’s premises, and Shivaji Maharaj emerged victorious. The famous duel between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan took place in the "Afzal Khan’s Hall," a structure within the fort. According to legend, Shivaji Maharaj used his quick wit and agility to defeat Afzal Khan, killing him in a surprise attack.
This victory at Pratapgad significantly boosted Shivaji Maharaj's reputation and established his dominance in the region.

The architecture of Pratapgad Fort is a blend of traditional Maratha design and military engineering. The fort is divided into two main sections: the upper fort and the lower fort. The upper fort, also known as the “Bale Killa,” is the highest point of the fort and houses the main fortifications. It is surrounded by steep cliffs on all sides, making it difficult for enemies to approach.
The lower fort contains the entrance gate, a series of temples, and the administrative buildings used by Shivaji Maharaj.
The lower fort also houses the "Afzal Khan’s Tomb," where the body of the defeated Bijapur commander was buried.
