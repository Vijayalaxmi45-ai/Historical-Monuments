Chittorgarh Fort

"A symbol of Rajput valor, pride, and sacrifice echoing through centuries."

Chittorgarh Fort, located in Rajasthan, is one of the largest and most historically significant forts in India. It served as the capital of the Mewar kingdom and witnessed numerous acts of bravery and sacrifice. Built on a massive hill, the fort features sprawling ramparts, gates, palaces, temples, and towers that narrate tales of Rajput valor.

The fort is home to landmarks like the Vijay Stambh (Victory Tower), Rani Padmini’s Palace, and the Meera Temple. Its architecture and layout reflect the glory of Rajputana, while its history is deeply rooted in the stories of resistance against Mughal invasions, especially during the siege by Emperor Akbar in 1567.
