Gateway of India

"An arch that welcomed emperors and witnessed the rise of a nation."

The Gateway of India, located in Mumbai, is one of the most iconic monuments of colonial-era India. Built in 1924, this grand arch was constructed to commemorate the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary. Standing tall at 26 meters, it blends Indo-Saracenic, Islamic, and European architectural styles, symbolizing both imperial grandeur and cultural fusion.

Facing the Arabian Sea, the Gateway became the ceremonial entrance for British governors and viceroys. Ironically, it also marked the symbolic end of British rule when the last British troops exited through it in 1948. Today, it remains a popular tourist destination and a powerful reminder of India’s colonial past and its stride into independence.
