Jagannath Temple

"A symbol of devotion, history, and divine architecture."

Jagannath Temple is a renowned Hindu temple located in Puri, Odisha. It is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Vishnu. This sacred temple is a pilgrimage site and is widely known for its annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival), attracting millions of devotees each year.

The temple was established in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodagangadeva. It is famous for its architecture and the unique wooden idols of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra, which are periodically replaced during the Ratha Yatra.
