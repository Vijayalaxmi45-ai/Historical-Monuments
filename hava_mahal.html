Hawa Mahal

"The Palace of Winds – A masterpiece of Rajput architecture."

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is an iconic five-story palace located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, it was designed to allow royal women to observe everyday life on the streets below without being seen. Its name reflects the palace's clever natural ventilation, allowing cool air to flow through its 953 small windows, or jharokhas.

The palace is made from pink sandstone and follows a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles. Shaped like Lord Krishna’s crown, its honeycomb-like façade features intricate latticework, decorative arches, and small balconies, making it a masterpiece of both beauty and function. It remains a top attraction in Jaipur and a proud symbol of Rajasthan’s royal heritage.
