Ajanta and Ellora Caves

"A journey through rock and time, where art, culture, and faith converge."

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are some of the most significant examples of ancient Indian rock-cut architecture and paintings. Located in Maharashtra, they represent a rich cultural, religious, and artistic heritage that spans centuries. While Ajanta is famous for its exquisite Buddhist paintings, Ellora stands out with its grand temples and sculptures. Together, they offer an insight into India's historical, spiritual, and artistic evolution.

Both sites are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and are considered among the most important monuments of ancient Indian architecture and art.
