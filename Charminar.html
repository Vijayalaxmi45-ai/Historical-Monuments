Charminar

"A historic icon of Hyderabad, blending culture, architecture, and legacy."

Charminar is one of the most iconic landmarks of Hyderabad, Telangana. It was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the fifth Sultan of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. The monument is known for its remarkable architecture and historical significance, particularly in the founding of Hyderabad.

The Charminar is a square-shaped structure with four towering minarets that rise to about 48.7 meters (160 feet) in height. It is an excellent example of Indo-Islamic architecture, incorporating Persian elements. The name "Charminar" is derived from two words: "Char" meaning four and "Minar" meaning towers.
