Hampi

"Where ruins whisper tales of a glorious empire."

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Karnataka, India. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, it is known for its grand temples, stone chariots, and intricate carvings. The town flourished between the 14th and 16th centuries and was a center of culture, trade, and spirituality.

Among its highlights are the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with its iconic Stone Chariot, and the Hazara Rama Temple known for its detailed Ramayana carvings. Today, Hampi stands as a breathtaking ruin that echoes the greatness of a bygone era.
