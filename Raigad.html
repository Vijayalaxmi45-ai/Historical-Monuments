Raigad Fort

"The Stronghold of the Marathas, a monument of pride and military brilliance."

Raigad Fort is a historic fort situated in the Sahyadri mountains of Maharashtra, India. At an elevation of approximately 1,350 meters, it offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and the Konkan coast. It was once the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and the site of his coronation in 1674.

The fort is an architectural masterpiece and a symbol of Maratha military strength. The Hirkani Buruj, a watchtower named after a woman who defied the steep cliffs, adds to the fort's charm. Raigad Fort remains a key historical landmark and a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.
