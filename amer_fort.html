Amer Fort

"A majestic blend of Rajput pride and Mughal elegance."

Amer Fort (also spelled Amber Fort) is a stunning fortress-palace located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built in the 16th century by Raja Man Singh I, it showcases a remarkable fusion of Rajput and Mughal architecture with its sprawling courtyards, intricate carvings, and grand gateways.

Overlooking Maota Lake, the fort is known for landmarks like the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Diwan-e-Aam, and Ganesh Pol. It was a royal residence and served as a symbol of power and culture in the region. Today, Amer Fort remains one of India’s most visited historical sites and a proud icon of Jaipur’s royal legacy.
