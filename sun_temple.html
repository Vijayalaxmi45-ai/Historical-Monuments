Sun Temple – Konark

"A stone chariot that rides through time, powered by the sun."

The Sun Temple of Konark, built in the 13th century by King Narasingha Deva I, is a monumental tribute to Surya, the Sun God. Located in Odisha, it is famed for its chariot-shaped design with 12 massive wheels and 7 stone horses, symbolizing time and celestial motion.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site reflects the brilliance of ancient Indian engineering and artistry. The temple's carvings depict mythology, daily life, and cosmic symbolism. Though the main idol is lost, the structure still radiates spiritual and historical significance.
