Red Fort

"A majestic symbol of Mughal glory and India's journey to freedom."

The Red Fort in Delhi is a grand 17th-century Mughal fortress built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638. Made of red sandstone, it served as the imperial residence for nearly 200 years and remains a powerful symbol of India's rich past.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site blends Persian, Timurid, and Indian architecture. The fort witnessed key historic events—from the British colonization to India’s independence, where the first national flag was hoisted by Jawaharlal Nehru in 1947.
