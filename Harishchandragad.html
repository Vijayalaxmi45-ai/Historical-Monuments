Harishchandra Gad

"A mystical fort in the Sahyadris where history and adventure meet the clouds."

Harishchandra Gad is a historic hill fort nestled in the Sahyadri ranges of Maharashtra. Known for its rugged terrain and breathtaking views, it offers trekkers a thrilling path through forests and rocky slopes. Believed to be built during the 6th century, it was later strengthened by the Marathas and holds deep mythological significance, often linked to the legendary King Harishchandra.

The fort is home to notable spots like the Harishchandra Peak, the ancient Kedareshwar Temple, and nearby Rui Caves. The surrounding villages preserve Maharashtrian culture and hospitality, making the journey not just a trek but a cultural experience. Accessible from Nashik, Harishchandra Gad remains a favorite destination for both history lovers and nature seekers.
