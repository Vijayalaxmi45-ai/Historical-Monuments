Torana

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. Situated about 15 kilometers southeast of the city of Pune, it is one of the earliest forts built by the Maratha rulers and holds great historical significance.

Torna Fort stands at an elevation of 1,403 meters (4,603 feet) above sea level and is part of the Sahyadri mountain range.

The fort is often considered the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, which laid the foundation for his empire. Torna Fort is renowned for its strategic location, commanding a view over the surrounding valleys and hills.

This makes it an ideal location for surveillance and defense, as it allows control of the surrounding areas and trade routes. The fort's construction is believed to have been initiated by the Rashtrakutas in the 13th century, though it was later captured and strengthened by Shivaji Maharaj. The fort's significance is further enhanced by the fact that it was one of the first forts to be taken by Shivaji in his early years, cementing his reputation as a fierce and capable military leader. It was captured in 1643 when Shivaji was just 16 years old. The fort was originally under the control of the Bijapur Sultanate before it was taken over by the Marathas. Torna Fort became a vital part of Shivaji Maharaj’s early military campaigns. After its capture, the fort was used as a strategic base for further military activities. Torna Fort is famous for its massive size and imposing structure. The fort spans a vast area and features various fortifications, including strong walls, bastions, and watchtowers. Torna Fort is known for its tall and sturdy gates, which once served as the main entrances. The main entrance is known as the “Kalyan Darwaza,” which was used to enter the fort and provides a glimpse of the fort's grandeur. The fort also has several secondary gates and a system of inner walls that protected the fort from external threats. The architecture of the fort is a mix of indigenous and military engineering, showcasing the brilliance of Maratha architects. One of the most important features of Torna Fort is its massive ramparts. These fortifications were built to withstand attacks and defend the fort against enemies.

The fort is located on a hilltop and is surrounded by steep cliffs, adding to its natural defense.

The fort was designed to be self-sufficient with water reservoirs, storage rooms, and barracks to house soldiers.


Torna Fort also has several temples, including one dedicated to Lord Hanuman. These temples serve not only as places of worship but also as cultural and spiritual centers for the people of the region. The fort also has remnants of old buildings and military structures that were once used for storage, troop accommodation, and defense operations. The fort’s historical significance is further highlighted by its role during the Maratha Empire. It was part of a larger network of forts that Shivaji Maharaj built and captured to strengthen his control over the western Deccan region. Torna Fort was crucial in providing shelter and serving as a base for Maratha operations in the surrounding areas. The fort’s location also made it a critical link in the Maratha defense network, as it provided a stronghold to protect Pune and its neighboring regions. The fort was further reinforced and expanded during the reign of Shivaji Maharaj.
