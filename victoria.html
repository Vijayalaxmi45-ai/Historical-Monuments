Victoria Memorial

"A timeless symbol of British grandeur, carved in marble."

Victoria Memorial is a stunning white marble monument in Kolkata, built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria. Designed by Sir William Emerson and commissioned by Lord Curzon, it blends British, Mughal, and Venetian architecture beautifully.

Made from the same Makrana marble as the Taj Mahal, it features domes, pillars, arches, and elegant carvings. Surrounded by lush gardens, it now serves as a museum celebrating India's colonial history and artistic heritage.
