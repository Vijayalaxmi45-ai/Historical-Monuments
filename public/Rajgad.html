Rajgad Fort

"A symbol of Maratha strength and heritage, Rajgad stands as a tribute to Shivaji Maharaj's legacy."

Rajgad Fort, located in the Sahyadri mountain range in Pune, Maharashtra, is a historically significant landmark. Perched at an elevation of 1,400 meters (4,600 feet) above sea level, the fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Rajgad served as the capital of the Maratha Empire for 26 years, from 1645 to 1670, before Shivaji Maharaj moved his capital to Raigad. It was a key military base during Shivaji's campaigns, showcasing the fort's role in the rise of the Maratha Empire.
