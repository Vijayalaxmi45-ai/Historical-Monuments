Rani Ki Vav

"A stepwell that blends love, legacy, and architecture into stone."

Rani Ki Vav is a majestic 11th-century stepwell in Patan, Gujarat, built by Queen Udayamati in memory of King Bhima I. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it reflects devotion, engineering, and beauty.

With its 7 sculpted levels and over 1,000 intricate carvings of Hindu deities, this inverted temple stands 64 meters long and 27 meters deep. It’s a symbol of Solanki dynasty brilliance and sacred craftsmanship.
