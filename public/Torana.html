Torna Fort

"A towering symbol of Maratha power and strategic brilliance."

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. Standing at an elevation of 1,403 meters (4,603 feet) above sea level, it offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding valleys and hills. The fort is one of the earliest forts built by the Maratha rulers and is historically significant for its role in the rise of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

The fort was captured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1643, at the age of 16, marking a key moment in the establishment of the Maratha Empire. The fort, originally controlled by the Bijapur Sultanate, became one of the first strongholds captured by Shivaji, cementing his reputation as a military leader. Its location and architecture made it a critical base for Maratha operations in the western Deccan region.
