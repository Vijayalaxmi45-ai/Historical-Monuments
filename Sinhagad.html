Sinhagad Fort

"A symbol of Maratha valor and bravery."

Sinhagad Fort is located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India, at a height of 1,312 meters above sea level. Originally known as Kondana, the fort is renowned for its association with the Maratha Empire and the heroic Tanaji Malusare.

Famous for the Battle of Sinhagad fought in 1670, this fort has a deep emotional significance in Maratha history. The battle saw the legendary Tanaji Malusare, a commander in Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's army, heroically scaling the fort's cliffs and leading the attack, ultimately sacrificing his life.

After Tanaji's death, Shivaji Maharaj renamed the fort to Sinhagad, meaning "Lion's Fort", in honor of Tanaji’s bravery and sacrifice.
